Cabo shark Dive is one of the first companies discovering and monitoring this amazing natural event, for OVER 10 YEARS!. We know perfectly how the marlin behave and how to find the best action and the biggest static bait balls. This is a unique opportunity to live a real wild life experience, like in a National Geographic documentary, face to face with one of the fastest and most elusive pelagic predators, hunting free in his natural environment!

A UNIQUE ECOSYSTEM, ONE OF THE WORLD BEST MARINE RESERVE WITH AN INCREDIBLE BIODIVERSITY

Magdalena Bay is a natural reserve in the Pacific Coast of Baja California Sur, not open yet to mass tourism. It is a 50 km long bay along the western coast of the Mexican state of Baja California Sur. It is protected from the Pacific Ocean by the sandy barrier islands of Isla Magdalena and Isla Santa Margarita. This bay is particularly noted for the seasonal migration of the California Grey whales and now also for the biggest striped marlin migration of the animal world. The abundance of sharks, billfishes, pelagic predators and different species of marine mammals makes Magdalena Bay the best place in Baja California Sur to free diving and snorkeling with marine mega - fauna.

WILDLIFE INSIDE AND OUTSIDE THE OCEAN

Nature is wild also outside the water. Nearby mangrove swamps provide sanctuaries for seabirds and an endemic population of COYOTES. More than 100 species of birds call the bay and the surrounding area. The bay includes the small and authentic Mexican fishing village of San Carlos, which will be our base. This is a place where time seems to be stopped, preserving nature and culture. Isla Magdalena and the surrounding area is also home to more than 8 endangered, endemic and rare plant species.

DIVING AREA

The activity occurs offshore, in the blue waters of the Pacific Ocean far from coastal recreational activities. We will go out early in the morning in the open ocean looking for any surface activity like birds hunting or bait balls, the unmistakable sign of presence of the mighty striped marlin hunting sardines! Then we will jump in the water for the big show! Visibility 100ft, water temperature 80-85 F
